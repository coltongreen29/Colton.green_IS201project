The Legend of Rory at Augusta

Rory McIlroy entered the 2026 Masters as the defending champion, having finally conquered Augusta National in 2025 to complete his career Grand Slam. This time, he did something even rarer — he defended the title, becoming only the fourth golfer in history to win back-to-back Masters titles, joining Jack Nicklaus (1965–66), Nick Faldo (1989–90), and Tiger Woods (2001–02).

The tournament was a rollercoaster from start to finish. McIlroy built a historic six-stroke halfway lead, only to see it evaporate in a dramatic final round before holding on with a gritty 1-under 71 to win at 12-under par.

"I just can't believe I waited 17 years to get one Green Jacket and I get two in a row." — Rory McIlroy

About Rory McIlroy

Rory McIlroy (born May 4, 1989, in Holywood, Northern Ireland) is widely regarded as one of the greatest golfers of his generation. He turned professional in 2007 and quickly rose to the top of the world rankings.

With his 2026 Masters victory, McIlroy now holds 6 major championships: two Masters titles (2025, 2026), two US Opens (2011, 2024), one Open Championship (2014), and one PGA Championship (2012).
